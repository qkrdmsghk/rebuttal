Ablation study stratified by perturbation effect size on K562 (n=272 perturbations). Perturbations grouped by fraction of DEGs (FDR < 0.05): <5% (n=92, weak), 5–10% (n=80, moderate), >10% (n=100, strong). Three conditions: AdaPert (full model), w/o non-DEG loss, w/o context. Top row: mean ± std. Bottom row: median ± IQR. Left to right: Pearson Delta, Discrimination L1/L2/Cosine. AdaPert's advantage is most pronounced on weak perturbations (<5% DEGs): median Pearson Delta +6.7% over w/o context. For strong perturbations (>10%), full AdaPert shows +12.3% median Discrimination L1 and +13.3% median Discrimination L2 over w/o context, indicating that context extraction is critical for discriminating large perturbation effects. The non-DEG loss contributes moderate gains across all groups, consistent with its role in suppressing mean-collapse on non-DEG genes.
